Efficient Speech Enhancement with Neural Homomorphic Synthesis Online Supplement

Authors

Wenbin Jiang, Tao Liu, Kai Yu

Abstract

Most neural network based speech enhancement methods work directly on time-frequency representations or learned features without employing the speech production model. This paper proposes an efficient speech enhancement method based on neural homomorphic synthesis. We decompose the speech signal into excitation and vocal with real cepstrum analysis, use two convolutional recurrent neural networks to estimate the target magnitude spectrum of the decomposed components, and synthesize the time-domain minimum phase signal from the estimated components. Finally, the enhanced speech is obtained by the synthesized minimum phase signal and the original noisy phase. Experimental results demonstrated that the proposed method yields a comparable performance with the state-of-the-art complex-valued neural network based method. In addition, we conducted extensive experiments and found that the proposed method is more efficient with a compact model.

Datasets

  • The Chinese Mandarin Female Corpus and the DEMAND are used for demo.
  • The noisy mixtures are composed by mixing the two corpora with SNR ~ U[-5,10] dB.
